- The RHS rows are the scientifically relevant Q-DESN rows for interpretation.
- The table remains useful because it shows why shrinkage choice matters; the
  text should make clear that ridge rows are included as baselines, not as
  recommended specifications.

## Freeze Decision

Freeze the current article-facing table after visual inspection in Overleaf.
The table is reproducible, source-pinned, and honest about the c13 VB refresh
and historical exDQLM/DQLM MCMC rows.

Do not edit the table again unless one of these checks fails:

- Overleaf compilation fails;
- the table/caption/provenance note is visually unreadable;
- a path/hash guard fails when regenerating the table;
- a factual claim in the surrounding text contradicts the generated evidence.

## MCMC Refresh Interpretation

The exDQLM/DQLM c13 MCMC refresh is complete and article-facing. Relative to
the earlier article MCMC rows, it improves fit RMSE in all 18 cells, forecast
MAE in 16 of 18 cells, and check loss in 17 of 18 cells. It should therefore
replace the historical MCMC baseline in the manuscript table.

The refresh does not imply that MCMC uniformly dominates VB. In the current
matched c13 evidence, MCMC improves same-variant VB check loss in only a small
subset of cells. The manuscript should describe the table as a matched
current-best comparison and avoid claiming a general MCMC advantage.
